About Me

Hi, my name is Richard Chen. I'm currently a sophomore at UC Berkeley studying Computer Science and Physics.

I have many years of experience working in laboratory and research settings. I have previously worked in labs such as the Space Power and Propulsion Laboratory at University of Maryland, College Park, the National Institute of Standards and Technology, and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory with Dr. Akito Kusaka on the POLARBEAR2 experiment. I am currently working with Dr. Paul Fallon at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory on the GRETINA experiment investigating the efficiency of HPGe detectors.

In 2017, I was honored as a Regeneron Science Talent Search semifinalist for my paper on the research I conducted at the Space Power and Propulsion Laboratory at UMD.

I have coded for many years and am well-acquainted with OOP languages such as Java and Python. I have experience with languages such as ROOT, SQL and React-Native as well.

In my free time, I love playing basketball, tennis, the violin, and enjoy taking photographs. Feel free to peruse my website!
